Forecast Logic

How each product's budget, impressions, and performance estimates are calculated — with the assumptions, benchmarks, and higher education context behind every number. Use this to confidently answer client questions about methodology.

📐 General Principles

Impression-based products (Display, Video, DOOH, Social, Contextual, Audience, Retargeting) calculate budget as: impressions ÷ 1,000 × CPM. Budget drives impressions — the rep enters a budget and the tool shows what it buys.

Lead-based products (SEM, PMax, Lead Gen) calculate: budget ÷ effective CPC = clicks → clicks × conv rate = leads. The budget recommendation is derived from a target lead volume rather than a fixed CPM.

Service products (SEO) price on a weighted program score and engagement multipliers rather than media metrics.

All impression volumes are rounded to the nearest 1,000. All budgets are rounded to the nearest $500 (impression products) or $5,000 (SEO).

Minimums exist on every product. The tool enforces these silently — inputs below minimum automatically floor to the stated minimum.

🔍 SEM + Keyword Retargeting — Blended Lead Generation
Effective CPC

Base CPC is set per program cluster (e.g. Nursing: $28, MBA: $35, Tech/CS: $25) reflecting real higher-ed keyword auction benchmarks. Four multipliers adjust it: competition (Low 0.70×, Mid 1.00×, High 1.50×), brand CPC (Low 1.30×, Mid 1.00×, High 0.75×), cohort (UG 0.85×, Grad 1.25×, Adult 1.10×, Military 0.75×), and degree level (Certificate 0.75×, Bachelor's 1.00×, Master's 1.30×, Doctoral 1.50×). These compound multiplicatively.

Effective CPC = baseCpc × compMult × brandCpcMult × cohortMult × degreeMult

Conversion Rate

Base conversion rate (click → inquiry/lead) is set per cluster (e.g. Nursing: 7.5%, Military: 8.0%, Liberal Arts: 6.0%) reflecting higher-ed landing page benchmarks. Brand strength and cohort/degree also affect conversion — a high-brand institution converts better because of stronger landing page trust and recognition. Multipliers: brand (Low 0.75×, Mid 1.00×, High 1.30×), cohort (UG 1.00×, Grad 1.15×, Adult 1.10×, Military 1.25×), degree (Certificate 1.35×, Bachelor's 1.00×, Master's 1.10×, Doctoral 0.90×).

Conv rate = baseConv × brandConvMult × cohortConvMult × degreeConvMult

Budget Recommendation

The tool targets 15 leads/month as a meaningful campaign floor. Required SEM media = (15 leads ÷ conv rate × effective CPC) ÷ SEM split (70%). This is the minimum to run a credible campaign — reps should scale up for higher enrollment goals. The recommendation auto-updates as any input changes.

Media Split & Fee Structure

Total media budget splits 70% to SEM (Google Search) and 30% to Keyword Retargeting. The 30% agency fee applies to SEM media only — KW RTG is priced at flat $16 CPM with no management fee, since it runs as programmatic display with minimal active management. SEM gross = SEM media ÷ 0.70. Package gross = SEM gross + RTG media.

Keyword Retargeting Pool

The RTG audience is built from SEM non-converters — people who clicked a paid search ad but did not submit a form. This is a higher-quality audience than generic retargeting because intent has already been demonstrated. Pool = SEM clicks × (1 − conversion rate). RTG leads are modeled at 0.2% CTR on display impressions × 35% of SEM's conversion rate (display converts lower than search, but the audience is pre-qualified).

Geographic Capture Rates

National search volume per cluster is derived from keyword category benchmarks. Geographic scope captures a fraction: Single DMA varies by market size (Large metro 7.0%, Mid metro 4.5%, Small metro 2.2%), State 9%, Multi-DMA 14%, Regional 25%, National 100%. A single DMA's capture rate depends heavily on market size — NYC and Omaha are both "one DMA" but differ 3× in search volume.

🎯 Lead Generation Package — Multi-Channel Forecast
Channel Split Logic

The recommended split by cohort: UG (SEM 40%, Social 25%, PMax 20%, RTG 15%), Grad (SEM 45%, Social 20%, PMax 20%, RTG 15%), Adult (SEM 40%, Social 25%, PMax 20%, RTG 15%), Military (SEM 50%, Social 20%, PMax 15%, RTG 15%). UG skews more social because traditional undergrad audiences discover programs through social feeds. Grad and Military skew search-heavy because those audiences search with more specific intent. Reps can override with the sliders — the forecasted leads update live.

Social Channel Model (Meta & LinkedIn)

Social leads are modeled from impressions → clicks → conversions. Each platform has a base CPM (Meta $18, LinkedIn $28) and base CTR (Meta 1.0%, LinkedIn 0.7%). Cohort-specific multipliers adjust both: LinkedIn earns a 1.4× CTR and 1.5× conversion multiplier for Grad cohorts because professional-network targeting aligns strongly with graduate program intent. Meta earns a 1.2× CTR multiplier for UG. Base social conversion rate (click → lead form) is 2.2–3.0% by cohort, lower than SEM because social is interruption-based rather than intent-driven. Social budget splits evenly across selected platforms.

Social leads = (budget ÷ CPM × 1000) × CTR × conv rate

Performance Max Model

PMax is modeled as an extension of SEM efficiency — it uses Google's automated audience expansion to reach people SEM misses, at a 1.30× CPL premium over SEM. The premium reflects Google's automation fee and the inherent efficiency loss from AI-driven audience selection vs. hand-managed keywords. PMax leads = PMax budget ÷ (SEM CPL × 1.30).

Fee Structure

SEM and PMax both carry a 30% agency fee (active bid management, keyword strategy, QA). Social carries a 30% fee (campaign management and creative direction). KW Retargeting is flat $16 CPM with no fee. The gross cost per channel = media ÷ (1 − fee rate). Package total = sum of all channel grosses.

🎓 Audience Targeting — Residential Display

Budget is driven by matched audience size and recommended frequency. The rep enters a list size; a 55% default match rate converts names to device-level matched households. CPM is $13 for lists ≥1,000 or $15 for smaller lists. Social add-on (+$3 CPM) requires 5,000+ records to unlock at scale. CTV add-on adds a blended $25 CPM component.

Frequency recommendations are auto-calibrated by matched audience size: smaller audiences need higher frequency to achieve meaningful recall (e.g. <500 matched → recommend 7×/mo), larger audiences benefit from broader reach at lower frequency (30k+ matched → recommend 10×/mo). The tool warns if budget is too low to deliver the recommended frequency.

Impressions = matched audience × frequency/mo × months. Budget = impressions ÷ 1000 × CPM

Minimum 250,000 impressions for base; 500,000 with add-ons. The 250K floor exists because programmatic display needs a volume floor to achieve statistically meaningful delivery and optimization.

🔁 Retargeting Suite — Three Tactics
Site Retargeting ($16 CPM)

Pool is modeled from institution profile: base pool by enrollment size tier (e.g. 8k–20k students → 20,833 monthly visitors), adjusted by affiliation (public 1.30×, private-faith 0.85×), brand strength (low 0.65×, high 1.45×), and regional presence (low 0.70×, high 1.30×). Frequency is also brand-driven: low-brand schools set 20×/mo (need more touches to build recall), high-brand schools use 12×/mo. Minimum 1M impressions, 12-month minimum flight.

Keyword Retargeting ($16 CPM)

Pool is based on number of programs × 155,000 monthly searchers base (per-program national keyword search estimate). The pool refreshes 30% with new searchers each month beyond the first: pool(month n) = programs × 155k × (1 + 0.30 × (n−1)). Frequency is 0.75 — reaching each unique searcher approximately 3× per 4 months. Positions KW RTG at roughly 40–50% of equivalent SEM spend. Minimum 250,000 impressions, 6-month minimum.

Site Behavioral Conquesting ($27 CPM)

Seed audience = visitors to competitor program pages. A behavioral lookalike model expands this 50–200×. Budget is determined by market scope: Local ($4,500/mo), Regional ($9,000/mo), National ($18,000/mo). Minimum 4 competitor institutions as seed, minimum 3-month flight to build statistical significance in the behavioral model.

📺 Video & Streaming — DMA/Goal-Driven Model

Budget is driven by market tier and campaign goal rather than impression inputs. Base monthly budget by DMA tier: Major ($10,000), Large ($5,500), Mid ($2,800), Small ($1,000). Goal intensity multiplier: Awareness 1.0×, Consideration 1.6×, Conversion 2.2×. More aggressive goals require more budget to drive meaningful action.

Channel allocation is recommended by audience × goal combination. For example, UG Awareness routes to TikTok 60% / Social Video 40% (where Gen Z audiences are). UG Conversion routes to YouTube 60% / CTV 40% (higher-intent contexts). Adult Conversion routes to CTV 60% / YouTube 40% (adult learners index higher on connected TV).

CPMs by channel: Audio $13, TikTok $19, Social Video $23, YouTube $27, CTV $55. Impression volume is derived from budget ÷ CPM × 1,000 per channel.

🏙 Digital Out of Home — Publisher Multiplier Model

DOOH is priced at $25 CPM on served impressions (what is purchased). But the actual audience reached is larger — DOOH screens are seen by multiple people simultaneously. Each venue type has a publisher multiplier representing the ratio of audience impressions to served impressions: Transit 5×, Billboard 7×, Campus 2.5×, Retail 4×, Healthcare 2.5×, Gym 3.5×.

The key client-facing number is publisher audience (served × multiplier), which represents total estimated exposure. This is the "headline" reach figure. Budget = served impressions ÷ 1,000 × $25 CPM.

Served impression volumes are set per venue type × market tier (e.g. Billboard in a mid-market = 250,000/month, Healthcare in a small market = 55,000/month). Minimum 250,000 served impressions.

📍 Location Targeting — Three Tactics
Blueprinting ($15 CPM)

Serves 3,333 impressions per location per month. This is derived from a 5-hour average daily dwell at the location × typical ad rotation frequency. Minimum 10 locations recommended; 3-month minimum flight.

Geo-Fencing ($12 CPM)

Impression volumes are category-driven, reflecting typical foot traffic: High Schools 15,000/location/mo, Community Colleges 15,000, Military Installations 35,000, Professional/Office 23,000, Public Spaces 30,000. The $12 CPM includes retargeting follow-up at no add-on cost — visitors are cookied and served ads for up to 30 days after visit.

Mobile Events ($500/event flat)

Flat rate per event — covers event-day mobile targeting and a 30-day follow-up retargeting window for all device IDs collected at the venue.

🔎 SEO — Weighted Program Portfolio Model
Pricing Formula

SEO investment is not priced per-page or per-keyword — it is priced on a weighted program score that reflects the enrollment value of each program tier. Priority programs count at 1.0×, Core at 0.6×, Emerging/Certificate at 0.3×. This weighting reflects that priority programs require deeper strategy, more content work, and more competitive effort per program than supporting tiers.

Weighted score = priority × 1.0 + core × 0.6 + emerging × 0.3
Year 1 = round(weighted × baseCPC × sizeMult × compMult, $5k) — floored at minimum

Base Rate by Engagement Depth

Foundational: $6,000/weighted program (lighter lift — audit, foundational on-page, core technical). Growth: $10,000/weighted program (strategic program work, content strategy, AI visibility). Performance: $15,000/weighted program (transformation-level, comprehensive, tied to enrollment outcomes). Engagement depth is the strongest single pricing lever in the model.

Multipliers

Institution size (Small 0.9×, Mid 1.0×, Large 1.2×) proxies for site complexity and coordination overhead. Program competitiveness (Low 0.9×, Medium 1.0×, High 1.2×) proxies for how hard it will be to win in search — competitive programs like MBA, Nursing, and Cybersecurity require more effort per page to break through.

Multi-Year Pricing

Year 2 = 75% of Year 1. Year 3 = 80% of Year 1. Year 2 is the lowest year because the foundational work is done and the effort shifts to optimization — a more efficient activity. Year 3 is slightly higher than Year 2 because the scale-and-adapt phase requires ongoing investment to respond to search algorithm changes and AI search evolution. Both Year 2 and Year 3 rates are applied to the raw (pre-floor, pre-rounding) Year 1 value to prevent rounding errors from compounding. Deal floors: Foundational $25k, Growth $50k, Performance $75k.

Year 1 Range (±10%)

The quote shows a ±10% range around Year 1 to reflect that final scope, technical complexity, and content volume are confirmed during kickoff. The midpoint is the recommended figure for proposals. Total contract range compounds Year 1's range with fixed Year 2/3 values.

Emerging Programs at Foundational Depth

Foundational depth caps emerging/certificate program activation at zero in Year 1. Accordingly, emerging programs are excluded from the price calculation entirely when Foundational is selected — the tool will not price work it cannot deliver. Upgrading to Growth unlocks emerging program pricing and activation (up to 2 programs in Year 1).

✦ Contextual Targeting — Category × Geo Model

Impression volume is set by geographic scope × number of categories selected. Base per-category monthly volumes: DMA 60,000, State 180,000, Regional 450,000, National 800,000. Delivery caps prevent oversaturation: DMA 200,000/mo, State 500,000/mo, Regional 1,000,000/mo, National 2,000,000/mo.

Audience × goal combinations drive recommended category selections (★ in the UI). For example, UG Apply routes to College Planning, Test Prep, Financial Aid, and Career categories — the highest-intent content environments for a traditional undergrad applicant. Military Learn More routes to Military, Career, and General News.

Frequency multipliers compound by goal (Learn More 1.0×, Visit 1.4×, Apply 1.8×) and audience (UG 1.0×, Grad 1.2×, Adult 1.3×, Military 1.1×). Higher-intent goals and harder-to-reach audiences require more impressions per outcome. $20 CPM (beta rate). Minimum 500,000 impressions.
